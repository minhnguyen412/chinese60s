COLLOCATIONS

有（一）点儿 means "slightly" (mostly used to refer to something unpleasant.)

有（一）点儿 + ADJ

The meaning of the interjection “啊” ("ah") varies with the change of its tones. When pronounced in the 4th tone it means "I see" or "Now I understand". For example:

啊，知道了。(Ah, I see!)

The modal particle “呢” is used at the end of an indicative sentence to indicate confirmation.

Both "两" and "二" mean "2", but they are used differently.

两:
1. 2 + 量词： 两个、两本、两件、两家
2. 2 + 千、万：两千、两万

二：
1. 1, 2, 3: 一、二、三、第二、二月、二楼
2. 2 + 十/百：二十、二百

When "多" is added after a numeral, it indicates the number is rather more than the stated number.

"不都是" expresses partial negation; "都不是" expresses total negation.

An indicative sentence becomes inquisitive if it is followed by "......好吗？", which indicates a suggestion or a request.

"咱们" includes both the speaker and the listener. "咱们" has two usages: first, both the speaker and the listener are included; second, the listener is not included.

The modal particle "吧" is used at the end of a sentence to lend it a suggestive, inquisitive or agreeing tone.

"很少" (seldom) and "不常" (not often) are synonymous.

"很少" can be used as an adverbial; "很多" cannot
