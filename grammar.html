GRAMMAR

There are no morphological changes in the strict sense in Chinese; the word order is the main grammatical constituent of the language.

主语：Subject - 谓语：Predicate - 名词：Noun - 动词：Verb - 形容词：Adjective - 状语：Adverbial - 宾语：Object - 定语：Attribute/Modifier - 补语：Complement

主语（定 + 名）谓语（状语 + 动 + 补 + 定 + 宾/形）

A sentence with a verb as its predicate is one in which the verb is the main element of the predicate. The grammatical order is:

主语 + 谓语（动词）+ 宾语

主语：Subject - 谓语：Predicate - 动词：Verb - 宾语：Object

In reading a cardinal number, no matter how many digits there are, they are read out one by one. For example: telephone numbers, house numbers, passport numbers, car numbers, etc

1. "一" is often read as "yāo"

2. "二" is read as "èr", and cannot be read as "liǎng"

3. Identical numbers are read out one by one

When inquiring about numbers, we say "几号" or "...号码是多少?"

A yes/no question is formed by adding the inquisitive modal particle "吗" to the end of an indicative sentence.

The modifying elements before a verb and adjective are called adverbials. Adverbs, adjectives, etc., can function as adverbials.

The adverbs "也" and "都" are placed before verbs and adjectives and function as adverbials.

Questions with interrogative pronouns, sometimes called wh-questions, refer to the questions with:

- 谁：who

- 什么：what

- 哪：which

- 哪儿（哪里）：where

- 怎么：how to

- 怎么样：how

- 几：how many

- 多少：how many, how much

Note: Interrogative sentences cannot take "吗" at the end except those formed with "declarative sentences + 吗". For example, it is wrong to say "你是哪国人吗？

Use interrogative pronouns to ask questions

The modifiers of nouns and nominal phrases are called attributes. The function of an attribute is to modify and define. In a phrase the attribute is placed in front of the noun; in a sentence before the subject or object, e.g.

Attribute (nouns; pronouns; very + adj) + 的 + nouns/nominal phrases

The structural particle "的" is placed immediately after the attribute. It is the formal indicator of the attribute.

1. When a noun or pronoun is used as an attribute to define, or to show possession and subordination, the particle "的" is added.

我的书 - 他的词典 - 老师的本子 - 图书馆的书 - 我们的老师

2. When an adjective phrase (very + adj) is used as the attribute, the particle "的" is added.

3. Sometimes the tructural particle "的" is not added between the attribute an the centre-word, e.g.

男同学 - 女同学 - 中文书 - 世界地图

Supply "的" in the proper place

The preposition "在" together with a location word and placed before a predicate verb, tells the place where an act occurred, e.g.

S + 在 + location word + predicate verb

The preposition "给" indicates the receiver or benificiary of an action, e.g.

给 + S.O + V

The classifier "些" indicates an uncertain amount; it is often used after "一", "哪", "这", "那", etc.

一/哪/这/那... + 些 + N

Note: The classifier "些" can only be used with "一". It does not collocate with other numerals.

When an adjective functions as the predicate of a sentence, we call it a sentence with an adjective predicate. This type of sentences are used to describe or evaluate someone, something, or a state of affairs.

S + (不）ADV + ADJ

In a sentence with an adjectival predicate, "是" can not be inserted between the subject and the predicate.

Adjectives in such sentences usually take an adverb before them. If they don't have an adverb, the meanings of the sentences will not be descriptive. Rather, they may imply a sense of comparison.

An affirmative-negative question is one in which the affirmative and negative forms of the main element of the predicate are paralleled, e.g.

S + V + 不 + V + N？

S + V + N + 不 + V？

S + ADJ + 不 + ADJ？

"的" phrase is formed by attaching the particle "的" to a noun, pronoun, adjective, verb or phrase. Its grammatical functions are equal to those of nouns, e.g.

NOUN/ PRONOUN/ ADJECTIVE/ VERB/ PHRASE + 的 = NOUN

When a subject-predicate phrase functions as the predicate of the sentence and explains or describes the subject, the sentence is called a sentence with a subject-predicate phrase as the predicate, e.g.

S + P (S'+P')

When the amount is estimated to be within 1 - 10, “几” is used; when it is more than 10, or cannot be estimated, “多少” is used.

Numeral-classifier compounds are used as attributives of the nouns to indicate the amount of things. Every thing has its corresponding unit of measurement (classifier) in Chinese.
